# ADR Review Checklist

Before finalizing or when critiquing an Architecture Decision Record (ADR), ensure it meets these standards:

## General
- [ ] Is the title clear, concise, and descriptive of the decision?
- [ ] Is the status marked correctly (Proposed, Accepted, Deprecated, Superseded)?
- [ ] Is the date present?

## Context
- [ ] Does the context clearly explain *why* a decision needs to be made?
- [ ] Is the problem statement objective and free of assumed conclusions?

## Options & Alternatives
- [ ] Are multiple viable alternatives listed? (If there's only one option, it's not a decision).
- [ ] Are the pros and cons of the rejected alternatives documented?

## Decision & Consequences
- [ ] Is the final decision explicitly stated?
- [ ] Are the *Positive Consequences* realistic?
- [ ] **Crucial:** Are the *Negative Consequences* explicitly documented? (Every decision has a trade-off. If there are no negatives, the ADR is incomplete).
